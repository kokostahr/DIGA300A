Goal Alignment

The main goal of my website is to showcase my work to potential employers and clients in a way that is visually appealing, organised and easy to navigate.

In my design, I have achieved this through:

  • Clean navigation that ensures users can quickly and easily access different sections of my website without confusion.
  • I have structured each section and each page (within my wireframe designs and hopefully html structure) in a way that naturally guides users to through my website, as explained above.
  • My work is organised and easy to access. The buttons and links are strategically placed to help users navigate and to direct users to relevant sections without having to search in a crazy way.
  • And again, the minimalist layout that keeps attention on the content, without too many distractions.

This aligns with my brand identity as I want to have a night sky aesthetic that makes browsing feel immersive, as if the users are looking up at the stars and discovering my work. My primary colour palette reinforces the celestial, night sky feel that also looks professional.

Style Process Documentation

Colour Palette Selection

I wanted my website to feel like a celestial experience, as if the users are browsing through the night sky. This is because my website is called Starfish Galaxy, so I focused on colours that reflect that kind of cosmic-like and dreamy aesthetic, while keeping everything clean and readable.

I go more in-depth about this in the Style Guides under the Design section. Here are the colours I chose:

  • Midnight Violet (#160649). This is the background colour; it represents the deep space â€¦yeah.
  • Royal Blue (#296ac8). This is used for the interactive elements. It provides a vibrant contrast and a sense of professionalism.
  • Lavender Blush (#cba1eb). Soft contrast to the royal blue, will be used for navigation bar, scrollbar background and the text in the footer, to keep things balanced and harmonious.
  • Soft Aqua (#92d2f5). Used for general text, adding a gentle, celestial glow while ensuring readability.

Before settling on this colour palette, I played around with pinks, deeper purples, and sea-themed colours. They clashed and readability looked terrible. So, I settled on a cooler, more harmonious balance of purples, blues and soft pastels.

My first wireframe sketches focused more on colour than ensuring the layout isnâ€™t overwhelming for users, and accessibility. They were blocky, too colour and basic with no annotation. I have refined them by adjusting the spacing, improving the hierarchy and ensuring visual balance. Overall, the refinements helped create a website that is both visually engaging and easy to use.

I have yet to finalise my font choices, but I do want to use fonts that evoke an ethereal yet simplistic feel. The typography should complement my night-sky aesthetic while maintaining readability and a minimalistic, clean design. The reason for having a clean design is because when we look up at the sky, there is cleanliness in the way the stars and things in space shine above our heads. It never feels negatively overwhelming. The sky always feels beautiful, structured and awe inspiring. To mirror this feeling, I am considering fonts like Raleway, Quicksand or Playfair Display as potential options.
