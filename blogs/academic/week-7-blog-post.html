Week Sevan: Reflection of South African Websites

by Star Nkosi

Published: 30 March 2025
Last updated: 14 April 2025

In this blog post Star looks at a South Africa nail beauty brand's website, and does a light analysis on the UI & UX pros and cons of the website...

Short Review on BLING GIRL.CO.ZA

Bling Girl is a south African beauty product…brand. Their products are of a high quality and really good. As an aspiring nail tech, I really recommend their products. But we’re here to look at their website. And their website… well… it’s a bit chaotic.

Just from the homepage, we can see that there is a lot going on. And my images do not show it, but these images that say “save 50%” flash and show all the different colours of the products available. This display is overwhelming and not necessarily intuitive for a user who lands on the site for the first time. I know I was very confused when using the website. LOOK at the comparison to another beauty brand, called Born Pretty. Look at how sleek and tidy their homepage is. There is a banner (which looks AI generated but anyways) and the navigation makes sense. As a user I know that the products displayed on the homepage are ‘trending’ products.

Anywhomst, let us take a look at Bling Girl’s products, and how they display the products.

When you click on the “All Products” link in the navigation bar, it brings you to a page where all the products are displayed and sorted into different categories. Many of the categories seem unnecessary. When you click on one category it expands and shows multiple options underneath that category. Such as the “BEGINNER SETS” that also shows a “KID SET” option. If you go through the website you’ll see that many of the extra categories have no products to display, and in my opinion it makes the navigation experience frustrating. Also as a person that knows what I am looking for (such as shopping for certain items) I must say that the way in which they have placed certain items makes no sense and seems redundant.

Under nails there is a sub-category called UV GELS that has 21 items on display. And the items displayed within this category could’ve been categorised into the other categories at the top, underneath “Gels&Acyrlic Powders”.

SEE?? It’s so messy and redundant. It feels like this website was not designed by people who know how to categorise nail products or… just any products.

Pros

The only pro of this website is that… it functions as a medium that allows users to purchase the relevant items they need.

Cons

The site is too cluttered, there is no aesthetic, there are too many visuals that aren’t placed in a way that does not overload the user (visual pollution), there is a LACK of consistency between the different webpages from the nav bar. AND to top it all off, the navigation SUCKS!
