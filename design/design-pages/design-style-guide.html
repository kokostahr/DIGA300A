Colour Palettes

Something that I want to maintain from my first wireframe designs (where I was exploring the potential colours of my website) would most definetly be the theme of the colour palettle. The name of my site is 'Starfish Galaxy', and I wanted to go for an acquatic theme in space, however, I am unsure of how I would mix colours associated with these two themes into one. So.. for now, I have decided to lean more towards the space theme. I want users to feel like they're browsing through the stars as they explore my site.

A gorgeous colour scheme, if I may say so myself. I believe my colour choices are sophisticate and visually striking, they adhere to the theme, while maintaining readability and accessibility. (PLEASE note that I have not tested how these colours would work together for colour blind users. When I speak of accessibility here, I mean in terms of readability for the users.) Midnight violet (#160649) as the background gives the website an elegant, professional starry night feeling. The royal blue (#296ac8) for the interactive elements, is bold, eye-catching and helps them stand out against the dark background. It makes the site feel lively without being too overwhelming. Lavender Blush (#cba1eb) is used as a soft contrast for the nav bar, scrollbar background and text within the footer. It gives the website a soft 'pop', preventing it from feeling too dark. Soft aqua (#92d2f5) will be used for text to keep things readable against the dark background and the other two saturated colours (also meant to represent the 'stars shining and glowing in the galaxy'). Here is how it would look when laid out:

There is a strong contrast between the colours I have chosen and the dark background. This will keep everything readable and accessible. The interactive elements pop, making sure that users can easily identify the interactive elements. There are some things I am considering though. I am worried that soft aqua wont be 'that' readable on the midnight violet background. In the test image above, it does seem like it will be readable, but I am curious about how it would look on the webpages that are very text heavy, like this webpage for instance.

Honestly, I was worried that my webpage may be a bit jarring, with the whole 'night sky' theme. What inspired me, and convinced me to continue with this idea was Bluefox Studio's Website Their site has that feel of exploring the stars/ galaxy off the bat, without even knowing what the content in the website is. I believe the dark background, and contrasting colours used for the text and other ui elements give it that feel.
